Securing Home Networks

In order to decrease the likelihood of viruses and unintended usage of your home network, there are a few ways that users can secure the points of entry into their network.

Using Strong Passwords

A common way that attackers can get into users' systems is by cracking users' passwords. Password requirements (ex. 10 characters minimum, includes special characters, etc.) have made passwords generally more secure. However, it is crucial that users are aware of measures they can take to ensure that their Wi-Fi password (or any password) is as secure as possible.

According to Google, passwords should follow these guidelines:
  • Passwords should be a combination of letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • Passwords for important platforms (ex. banking and email) should be different.
  • Longer passwords are generally more secure (Google recommends at least 12 characters).
  • Passwords should not contain personal information or common words.

Changing Default Admin Credentials

A large portion of home computer users do not change the default admin login of their home network. Because of this, attackers can easily gain access to home networks, simply by using default login Credentials (think "username" for the username and "password" for the password.)
