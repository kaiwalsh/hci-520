Troubleshooting

As with all technology, there are times when a home network may not be working as expected. When this happens, it's important to have a toolkit of possible fixes.

Troubleshooting a Home Network

It may sound clich√©, but turning your router on and off is a great first step in fixing a networking-related issue. Doing this resets the current state of the router back to normal, clearing out potential issues.

Outside of this, there are a few different things to check, including router placement, the number of devices on the network, and router settings. Network connectivity issues can also be caused by service isses coming from your Internet provider.

Troubleshooting Checklist

When trying to diagnose and fix an issue with a home network, here is a general checklist of things you can try:
  • Turning the router (and/or your device) off and on again
  • Moving to a different part of the house, to see if connectivity comes back
  • Turning off some Internet-connected devices, to see if performance improves
  • Checking for service interruptions from your Internet provider
